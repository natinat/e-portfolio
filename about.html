About me

Introducing my professional and academic background.

My Professional Background

I am a Principal Product Manager and AI strategist specialising in Conversational AI, agentic experiences, and Generative AI product development. At Booking.com, I lead key initiatives such as Conversational UI Foundations and play a central role in scaling unified AI adoption and innovation across the company’s products and verticals.

My work focuses on building reusable conversational capabilities, establishing architectural and UX foundations, and enabling teams across the organisation to deliver consistent, high-quality AI-powered experiences. I combine strong technical understanding with deep product intuition, helping multidisciplinary teams translate complex machine learning concepts into user-centric solutions and measurable business impact.

My Academic Background

My academic and professional trajectory began in graphic design and printing production, where I developed a strong foundation in visual communication and systematic problem-solving. Although I valued this early work, I became increasingly drawn to the technological dimensions of product creation. This led me to pursue independent study in web development and search engine optimisation, which initiated a transition into the technology sector. Through subsequent roles in several tech organisations, I progressively expanded my knowledge of digital systems, user experience, and product development. While advancing professionally, I completed a BA with The Open University to strengthen my theoretical grounding. Motivated by a desire to deepen my technical expertise and contribute more meaningfully to the field of artificial intelligence, I have recently returned to academia to pursue a postgraduate degree in Artificial Intelligence.

Product Leap AI

Alongside my corporate role, I run Product Leap AI, where I advise organisations on how to build their first AI MVPs and develop strategic approaches to AI adoption. I create practical frameworks, talks, and workshops—including themes such as the AI-augmented tech landscape and AI strategy vs AI skills—that guide teams through the opportunities and challenges of modern AI product development.
